Ultra-native to Gen-Z slang. Instantly understood. Domain-friendly, app-store-safe. The -r suffix (Flickr, Tumblr era) signals digital-native product. Memorable, spellable, and screams exactly what it does.

"smooth" — stylized — 5 chars
"always know exactly what to say."
Aspirational and confident. "Smooth operator" energy. Works for both genders, all orientations. Slightly older Millennial crossover — captures the broader audience without losing Gen-Z cool.
Flixx
flirt + flex + flix — 5 chars
"stop leaving people on read. start leaving them obsessed."
Double-x gives it logo punch. Association with "flex" (show off your game) and flirting. The flix suffix nods to content culture — feels native to TikTok. Edge: the double-x is highly memorable in thumbnails.

The Archetype

That one friend who always knows what to text back — witty, calm under pressure, never sweats it, always gets the reaction they want. They're not cheating, they're just built different.

What We're NOT

Not a creepy pick-up-artist app. Not a corporate wellness tool. Not a chatbot trying to sound human. Not preachy, not safe, not beige. Never apologetic about being direct.

Visual Reference

Midnight UI meets TikTok native. Think: Apple Notes dark mode × Figma dark canvas × Snapchat's bold color pops. Premium but anti-corporate. Designed to be screenshot-ready.

The Feeling

Opening the app should feel like calling your most charismatic friend at 11pm and asking "what do I say to this?" — instant confidence boost, no judgment, pure output.

05
Tone of Voice & Copy Rules
lowercase everything except proper nouns. makes the brand feel texty, native, not corporate. "get your reply" not "Get Your Reply"
never write "Our AI-powered solution leverages..." — sounds like a LinkedIn post. instant trust death.
use contractions. "you'll", "don't", "it's", "we've". reads like a person, not a FAQ page.
never apologize or hedge. no "this may not work for everyone" or "results may vary" in the UI.
short sentences. brutal clarity. "upload. pick a mode. send." 3 words beats 3 paragraphs.
don't explain the AI. don't say "using large language models" or "powered by GPT". nobody cares.
be outcome-specific. "wake up with a date" not "improve your communication skills".
no passive voice. "the message is generated" → "you get 3 replies in 8 seconds".
